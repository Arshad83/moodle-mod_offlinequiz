3. The answer form

The answer form contains a block for your name (A), a block for the identification number (B) and a block for the answers (C). Fill in the boxes for name, signature and identification number first. Attention: The machine will not try to interpret your handwriting after the examination. These boxes are only for documentation and correction.

Mark the boxes for the identification number carefully. Be aware that the computer will need these marks to identify your result. Mistakes in the block for the identification number can leed to delays and misinterpretation.

How is the computer assigning the answer form to the right person?

a)       The system is reading your name with an optical character recognition software.
b)       Your singature is scanned with the same software as finger prints in crime investigations.
c)       Only the crosses in the boxes under your identification number are interpreted.
d)       The system is reading your identification number with an optical character recognition software.
